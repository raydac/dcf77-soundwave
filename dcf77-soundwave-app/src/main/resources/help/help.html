DCF77 Signal Generator

This application is designed to emulate the long-wave time signals by generating it in the audible frequency range. It allows you to synchronize radio-controlled wristwatches using your computer's audio system. It supports DCF77, JJY, WWVB amd BCP signals.

DCF77: German longwave time signal broadcast from Mainflingen, used mainly in Europe.

JJY: Japanese time signal transmitted from two locations, covering Japan and nearby regions.

WWVB: U.S. longwave time signal from Colorado, widely used in North America.

BPC: Chinese time signal transmitted from Shangqiu, covering China and surrounding areas.

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Select the signal mode: Open the menu Mode and select the needed signal format.
  2. Select the audio output device: Open your systemâ€™s sound settings and make sure your computer is configured to use headphones or a similar audio output device. This will be the channel used to transmit the simulated signal.
  3. Configure the signal: In the application interface, choose the desired signal waveform (e.g., square or sine wave), set the modulation frequency, and select the appropriate carrier frequency for the signal emulation.
  4. Position your watch: Place the radio-controlled wristwatch directly onto the headphones. Make sure the back of the watch is in contact with the speaker membrane, as the audio signal will act as a magnetic field transmitter.
  5. Start signal generation: Press the Play button in the application to begin generating the signal.
  6. Wait for synchronization: Allow the signal to play continuously. Synchronization may take some time depending on the model and sensitivity of the watch. Typically, the process takes between 5 and 15 minutes.
  7. Confirm synchronization: Once the watch has received the signal, it should automatically adjust its time to the correct value. You may notice the hands moving or the digital display updating.

Please note that successful synchronization depends on various factors including watch model, signal quality, volume level, and how well the watch is positioned on the audio source. Experiment with these variables if the synchronization does not occur on the first attempt.
