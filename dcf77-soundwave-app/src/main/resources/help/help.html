DCF77 Signal Generator

This application emulates long-wave time-code stations by generating their patterns in the audible frequency range. You can use it to synchronize radio-controlled watches through your computer’s audio output. It supports DCF77, JJY, WWVB, and BPC formats (select the mode from the Mode menu).

DCF77: German longwave time signal broadcast from Mainflingen, used mainly in Europe.

JJY: Japanese time signal transmitted from two locations, covering Japan and nearby regions.

WWVB: U.S. longwave time signal from Colorado, widely used in North America.

BPC: Chinese time signal transmitted from Shangqiu, covering China and surrounding areas.

Optional: custom time, UTC, and time shift

The right-hand column has extra toggles beside waveform, sample rate, and carrier frequency. They change which instant in time is encoded into the signal; they do not switch the protocol (that remains whatever you chose under Mode).

  • Custom time ([): When enabled, you pick a fixed date and time in a dialog. The app then encodes that moment instead of the live clock (the main time panel turns a different color to show “fixed” mode). Turn it off to return to the live time source (see Settings → Time sources).
  • UTC: When enabled without custom time, the same clock instant as your selected time source is shown and used in UTC (the status line shows UTC instead of the signal’s usual zone, e.g. Europe/Berlin for DCF77). When custom time is on, UTC means your chosen date/time is interpreted and held in UTC. Use this when you want the encoded minute boundaries aligned with UTC rather than the station’s default civil time zone.
  • Time shift (+/−, labeled ➕/➖ on the button): Applies a constant offset to the time used for encoding (and for File → Save as…). After you press the button, choose sign (+/−) and an offset up to 24 hours in the dialog; confirm with OK. A non-zero offset appears in the status line as SHFT:±HH:MM. To clear the shift, press the button again to turn it off, or confirm the dialog with offset 0 (the toggle turns off if you cancel or leave offset at zero).

Tip: You can combine UTC and time shift with live or custom time. The order of effects matches what you see on the panel: the app applies your time-source/custom-time/UTC choice, then applies the shift when building each minute.

Step-by-step instructions

  1. Select the signal mode: Open the Mode menu and choose the format you need (DCF77, JJY, WWVB, or BPC).
  2. Select the audio output device: Use Settings → Output device and pick the line that corresponds to the headphones or cable you will use. Alternatively, use your operating system’s sound settings so the default output is the device you want.
  3. Configure the signal: In the application window, choose the waveform (sine, square, or triangle), the sample rate (44.1 / 48 / 96 kHz), and the carrier frequency offered for the current mode.
  4. Position your watch: Place the radio-controlled watch on the headphones so the back of the case sits on the speaker driver; the audio acts as a coupling path for the modulated tone.
  5. Start signal generation: Press the play button to start; press again to stop. While playing, some menus are disabled until you stop.
  6. Wait for synchronization: Leave the signal running. Depending on the watch model and volume, synchronization can take on the order of several minutes (often roughly 5–15 minutes).
  7. Confirm synchronization: When the watch has locked to the code, it should set itself to the time being transmitted. You may see hands move or digits update.

Success depends on watch model, volume, waveform choice, carrier choice, and mechanical coupling to the speaker. If nothing happens, try small changes to those factors before assuming a fault.

Recording to a file

Use File → Save as… to write a WAV file of several consecutive encoded minutes. The dialog asks how many minutes to render (1–60). The same time options (custom time, UTC, time shift) apply as for live playback.
